Understanding India's Three Crop Seasons

Each season brings unique conditions, crops, and opportunities for Indian farmers.

🌧️

Kharif Season

June – October

The monsoon crop season — sown at the start of the southwest monsoon and harvested after it withdraws. Relies almost entirely on rainfall.

Key Kharif Crops
🌾 Rice🌿 Cotton 🌽 Maize🫘 Soybean 🥜 Groundnut🌱 Bajra 🫘 Toor Dal🌶️ Chilli
Sowing starts with the first monsoon rains in June
Harvest between September and October
High humidity increases pest and disease pressure
Contributes ~60% of India's total food grain output
🌾

Rabi Season

November – March/April

The winter crop season — sown after the monsoon retreats. Depends on residual soil moisture, irrigation, and winter rains for growth.

Key Rabi Crops
🌾 Wheat🧅 Onion 🥔 Potato🫘 Chickpea 🌻 Mustard🧄 Garlic 🫘 Lentil🥦 Peas
Sown November–December after monsoon withdrawal
Cool dry winters ideal for grain quality
Needs 2–3 irrigations for most crops
Harvested in March–April before summer heat
☀️

Zaid Season

March – June

The summer crop season — a short-duration season between Rabi and Kharif, utilising residual irrigation. Good for fast-growing vegetables and melons.

Key Zaid Crops
🍉 Watermelon🥒 Cucumber 🌽 Summer Maize🫛 Moong Dal 🌿 Cowpea🥬 Leafy Veg
Short duration crops (45–75 days) are ideal
High temperatures accelerate crop growth
Irrigation essential — no monsoon support
Allows an extra income cycle each year

Seasonal Farming Tips

🌱
Soil Preparation
Deep plough 2–3 weeks before sowing to expose pests to sun, improve aeration, and incorporate last season's stubble as organic matter.
💧
Irrigation Timing
Water at critical growth stages — germination, flowering, and grain filling. Avoid waterlogging which causes root rot and yield loss.
🔄
Crop Rotation
Alternate cereals with legumes every season to fix nitrogen naturally, break pest cycles, and reduce fertilizer costs by up to 30%.
